# Project - Part 3

## Final Data Story
[Click here to access full data story](https://carnegiemellon.shorthandstories.com/triple-threat-in-borno/index.html)

## Summary
The goal of the story was to create awareness of the compounded humanitarian crisis in northeast Nigeria with a focus on Borno state – the state hit hardest by the crisis. The region is afflicted with insecurity due to violent attacks by an extremist Islamic group which has led to the displacement of millions of persons. With loss of livelihood and reliance on humanitarian assistance, a lot of these displaced persons end up in displacement sites and are mostly soley reliant on assistance. However, due to scarcity of resources, even very active actors such as the World Food Programme are constrained in delivering assistance to vulnerable people. Thus the need for more donations to help address the needs of vulnerable people and make life less daunting for them.

## Audience
The target audience for the story are male and female young to older adults who are socially concsious, supporters of change and inclined towards hunger alleviation. Since the call to action was a request for donations, that constrains the target audience to middle to high income earners. An ideal niche group would be West Africans since they are closer geographically and/or culturally to the issue but there really is no restriction of race or ethinicity or nationality for this story. 

## Changes from part 2
After receiving feedback from the user research & teaching team, I made the following changes from part 2 to part 3:
- Added a map of Nigeria with an outline of Borno state. I also included a comparison of Nigeria to the United States in terms of area size and population size. The goal was to not just show what the country looks like on a map but provide a familiar comparison.
- Adjusted titles and labels for all visualizations to be clearer and remove abbreviations. This was done to ensure the visualizations can standalone and be clearly communicative and easy to understand.
- Reviewed fonts in Shorthand page to enforce consistency. This was to improve the overall aesthetic of the story. Ensuring all quotes are in the same font and the headings are in same font and font size.
- Edited the call-outs/headings of the pages to support the story better and be sentence-like. This was to improve communicative power of the story and to imrpove explainability.
- Updated the pictures within the story to ensure copyright compliance. Photos from Unsplash were used. The pictures selected were both in black and white and were deliberately picked to invoke empathy in the reader. The cover picture is a close-up shot of a little girl holding a bowl to the side of her head and the picture on the donations page is a picture of two little boys with a dog.
- Included brief information on the insurgency in northeast Nigeria and included links at the end of the story for deeper exploration. This was to help provide context on the severity of the issue within the region.
- Included definitions of keywords that would be important while reading the story. This was to improve readability of the story for the audience.
- Updated the donate now page to show two donation links, one for the World Food Programme and another for the United Nations Refugee Agency. The goal here was to provide two options for donors – one for food and one for displacement.


## Post-presentation Insights and Reflections
The project process was quite enlightening. At ideation stage in part 1, it seemed like an important topic but complex topic to tackle for the project. Although it still remains important and complex, I think it was interesting doing the research and deciding what visualizations to create from the vast data that would support the narrative. I was more aware of my use of color, choice of visulaization and overall communication through the visualizations. With a goal of stimulating melancholic feelings in the reader – thus the use of a dark theme.  Utilizing Balsamiq, Flourish and Shorthand in this process was an insightful learning experience. For the presentation, it was quite difficult summarizing the story in under a minute but I do not think it is impossible.
