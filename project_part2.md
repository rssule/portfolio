## User Research and Interviews

### Target Audience
<p>My project is to raise awareness of the crisis/shadow pandemic in Borno state – hunger. My call to action is to get the general public to donate to the World Food Program to support their efforts in fighting the hunger emergency in Northeast Nigeria. My target audience would ideally be people who are socially conscious and inclined towards alleviating hunger but the audience is the general public. Specifically individuals between the ages of 24 – 50, both male and female, with at least a college degree, middle – high income earners. Nigerians and other West Africans would be a good niche audience since they are closer culturally and/or  geographically to the issue.</p>

<p>Since I am currently in Pittsburgh, mostly on campus and away from West Africans, I sought out individuals between the ages of 24-30 years, who currently earn income and are socially conscious. Fortunately, I was able to interview two West Africans in the diaspora.</p>

### Interviews
The main focus of my user research was aesthetics, intuitiveness, persuasiveness and overall perceptibility. The following questions were asked to gauge the story’s performance and gain opportunity for improvement.

**Aesthetics**
-	Do the pictures complement the story?
-	Are the visualizations confusing or unclear?
-	Is the choice of color suitable for the mood of the story?
-	What doesn’t work aesthetically?

**Intuitiveness**
-	Is the order of the story clear to you? Or should the information be reordered?

**Persuasiveness**
-	Did you learn anything new from this?
-	Did this inspire you to know more about the situation in Borno or about the issue in general?
-	Is there more information that could have been helpful in your understanding of the issue?

**Perceptibility**
-	Overall, can you understand the information with minimal effort?
-	Does this clearly communicate valuable information to you?
-	Do you have any suggestions that weren’t addressed in the previous questions?

### Improvements for Part 3

Based on feedback from the user interviews, here are changes to be implemented in part 3:

- Add a map of Nigeria, showing Borno and the Northeast region
- Adjust labels for all visualizations to be clear
- Check and enforce consistency of fonts
- Edit and include subtitles and call-outs to be explainable and support the story
- Include one or two more pictures that support the story
- Include some information on the insurgency in Borno
- Include definitions for onsite vs offsite, food insecure, unmet need/humnitarian need, IDP and any other helpful definition
- Change the donate now page (already adjusted in part 2)
